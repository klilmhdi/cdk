Chronic kidney disease

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a long-term condition where the kidneys don't work as well as they should. It's a common condition often associated with getting older. It can affect anyone, but it's more common in people who are black or of south Asian origin. CKD can get worse over time and eventually the kidneys may stop working altogether, but this is uncommon. Many people with CKD are able to live long lives with the condition

How common the CKD is in UAE and around the world?

CKD prevalence in the Middle East is estimated to be around 12 percent, on a par with Europe and Latin America yet in Africa this rate is just 8 percent. Sadly, In the UAE, CKD affects approximately 7 percent of the population. Globally, it affects approximately 10 percent of the world's population.

What are the causes of CKD?


High blood pressure

It can put strain on the small blood vessels in the kidneys and stop the kidneys working properly

High cholesterol

It can cause a build-up of fatty deposits in the blood vessels supplying your kidneys, which can make it harder for them to work properly

Diabetes

Extra weight forces the kidneys to work harder and filter wastes above the normal level

Obesity

It can damage the tiny filters in the kidneys

Who is the most affected by CKD?

CKD is more common in people aged 65 years or older (38%) than in people aged 45 – 64 years (12%) or 18 – 44 years (6%). CKD is slightly more common in women (14%) than men (12%). CKD is more common in non-Hispanic Black adults (16%) than in non-Hispanic White adults (13%) or non-Hispanic Asian adults (13%). About 14% of Hispanic adults have CKD.

How important it is to address the issue of CKD?

Early detection and treatment can often prevent CKD from worsening, If the situation worsens, it may lead to complete kidney failure forever!
